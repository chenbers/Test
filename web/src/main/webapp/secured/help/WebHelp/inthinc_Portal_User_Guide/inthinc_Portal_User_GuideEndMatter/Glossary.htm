Glossary

Click – indicates using a mouse to perform an action (ex. Click Save. The data is saved to your account.)

Crash Detection – A crash on the inthinc™ device is defined as an impact event whose acceleration data exceeds the hardware and software thresholds.

1.   The instantaneous impact must have an acceleration value of magnitude 4.5G or greater.

2.   The delta-V (change in velocity) over 128ms must exceed the software thresholds for one of the three axes. The software thresholds are currently 3.15mph for the X and Y axes and 5mph for the Z-axis.

Note: For crash detection to work, the tiwiPro device must be oriented.

Rollover Detection – A rollover on the tiwi™ device is defined as the presence of a constant acceleration of at least 0.7G for twenty seconds in any axis except for the downward axis. Tabularhe downward axis is the axis of gravity for a vehicle when it is parked.

Note: It is possible to have a crash and a rollover occur, so long as the criteria for each event are met.

Driving Event – an occurrence of speeding, aggressive driving, not wearing a seat belt or entering/leaving a zone area you set up. The tiwi™ device reports these items and they are displayed in the portal on the Reports page.

International Mobile Equipment Identity (IMEI) – the IMEI is a unique number assigned at manufacturing time to every GSM and UMTS cell phone device. This number is associated with the device, not the subscriber, and can be used by us, the manufacturer, to disable the device in the event it is stolen.

Press – indicates using a keyboard to perform an action (ex. Press Enter. The command executes.)

Select – indicates a series of items to be clicked in order to achieve an end goal (ex. Select Admin > Users > Add User.

tiwiPro™ Portal – an Internet access point where supervisors visit to build their fleet database and make associations between tiwi™ devices, vehicles, and drivers. Here they set up driver mentoring preferences as well. Supervisors and individual team members can log in to this portal to review their personal performance and compare it to their group statistics.

VIN – abbreviation for Vehicle Identification Number that identifies a specific vehicle. Typically the VIN is located on the lower driver side corner of the dashboard or on a sticker adhered to the panel inside the driver door.

Zone Alert – specified settings that notify you by telephone call, text message and e-mail when your driver enters or leaves a Zone. This is different from alerts you receive by default for speeding, aggressive driving and failure to wear a seat belt when driving at unsafe speeds.

Zone – a geographical area that you specify in order to track a driver’s arrival and departure within the zone. This helps you monitor driver movement into areas at specific times of day. For example, you may want to know if your driver leaves or arrives at a destination.
