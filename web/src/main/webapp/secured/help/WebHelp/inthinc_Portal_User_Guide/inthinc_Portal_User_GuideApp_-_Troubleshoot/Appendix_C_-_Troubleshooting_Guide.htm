Appendix C -    Troubleshooting Guide

Use the following table to quickly troubleshoot issues encountered in your inthinc® Portal system.

Table 1: Troubleshooting Guide

Category

Symptom

Possible Solution(s)

Device

Device lights aren’t on.

•   To power up, we use a wiggle sensor.  Depending on the type of vehicle the device may not turn on until you start moving the vehicle.  In most cases, opening the car door will turn the device on.

•   Verify that the device is plugged in to the OBD-II port. (For a list of common OBD port locations, see Appendix A.)

•   If the device is plugged in and the lights are still not on, unplug the device for at least 30 seconds, then reconnect the device. You should immediately have a green light (power). After 10-15 seconds you should have a blue light (satel­lite lock).

 
What does it mean if the green LED is flashing (on for one second, then off for one second)? .

•   The device is in Parent Mode. In Parent Mode the driver will not hear any audible alarms, but violations will still be sent to the portal. This mode is used for initial customer pilots to create a baseline. Call Tech Support.

 
Device has no blue light.

•   No blue light may indicate no satellite lock. Move vehicle to get clear line of sight to the southern sky (away from tall buildings and tall trees), then re-check.

 
Device won’t orient.

•   Vehicle needs to be on a flat surface and in an area that has a clear line of sight to the southern sky (away from tall buildings and tall trees).

•   The blue LED needs to be illuminated before you can orient the device. If the blue LED is not illuminated, you do not have satellite lock, and cannot orient the device.

 
When orienting device, device beeps instead of giving an audio
confirmation.

•   Micro SD Card may not be properly inserted or may be missing. Check slot closest to edge (opposite of power cord). Micro SD Card should sit well below the slot opening. If not, (1) disconnect the tiwi™ device, (2) press the card in using a key or paper clip, and then (3) reconnect the device.

 
Device has no audio.

•   If lights function on device, but there is no audio, it might be intentional. Contact Technical Support at 1-866-294-8637 ext. 3.

 
I pressed the Call button and nothing happened.

•   Vehicle may be out of cell coverage area.

•   Device may be defective.

 
I pressed Call button and got a voice mail instead of a person.

•   A custom E-Call phone number may not have been set up. Go to Admin > Devices, select a device, click Edit, enter E-Call phone number, and then click Save. Tip: Cell phone access can be unreliable. We recommend entering a landline when possible.

 
When changing devices out of vehicles, how do I clean the adhesive off?

•   On windshields, using rubbing alcohol on a cloth should work.

•   On plastic or vinyl surfaces, we recommend using a commercial gum removal product, such as Goo Gone ®.

 
When changing devices out of vehicles, how do I set up the device for the new vehicle?

•   Install the device in a new vehicle:, but do NOT turn on the ignition. The device is configured to communicate to the vehicle bus of the original vehicle and may not be compatible with the new vehicle. Tap the tiwi™ to get the device to boot.
(1) At the portal, create a new vehicle.
(2) Reassign the device to the new vehicle.
(3) On the portal, enter the VIN.
(4) Assign a driver to the device.
(5) Orient the device to ensure the VIN is registered
     to the device.

Vehicle
Where is the OBD port in the vehicle?

•   For a list of OBD-II port locations, see Appendix A. If your vehicle does not appear on the list, refer to the vehicle’s owners manual from your vehicle’s manufacturer.

Tip: OBD port is generally within 18 inches of the center of the steering wheel.

 
Non-critical vehicle dashboard issue: Seat Belt, Idling, RPM, or MPG status is not accu­rate.

•   (1) Compare VIN number in vehicle to VIN number entered in inthinc® Portal portal (go to Admin > Vehicles).
(2) If VIN numbers don’t match, edit the VIN on the vehicle’s page in the portal, then re-orient vehicle by pressing the Call button on the tiwi™ device.
If VIN numbers match, then it may be a hardware problem—Contact Techni­cal Support at 1-866-294-8637 ext. 3.

 
Critical vehicle dash­board systems are behaving strangely:

•   Dash lights aren’t on.

•   Gauges are making a sweeping motion.

•   A component module (Trac­tion Control, Security, Theft, etc.) is disabled.

•   (1) Unplug tiwi™ device from the OBD.
(2) Turn the vehicle Off and then On again.
(3) If engine starts, skip to the next step.
If engine does not start, pull ground terminal from battery, let it sit for 10 minutes, reconnect ground terminal, then restart vehicle.
(4) Compare VIN number in vehicle to VIN number entered in tiwi™ Portal (go to Admin > Vehicles).
(5) If VIN numbers do not match, edit the VIN on the vehicle page in the por­tal, then re-orient vehicle by pressing the Call button on the tiwi™ device.
(6) If VIN numbers match, it may be a hardware problem. Contact Technical Support at 1-866-294-8637 ext. 3.

 
Vehicle battery is dead.

•   It is possible that the device did not go into low power mode. To avoid having your vehicle battery drained, verify that the LEDs are not illuminated.

Note: Device’s LED’s will remain illuminated for 15 minutes before turning off and going into Low Power Mode.

•   Pay attention to where you park the vehicle because the device is extremely sensitive to motion. A strong gust of wind or vibration (such as parking close to a rail road track) can trigger the device to power on after powering down.

 
Aggressive Driving is really sensitive.

•   Adjust Aggressive Driving settings in tiwi™ portal:
(1) Go to Admin > Vehicles.
(2) Select the vehicle, then click Edit.
(3) Select the Speed & Sensitivity tab.
(4) Click and drag the slider arrow toward the “least” end for Hard Acceler­ate, Hard Brake, Hard Turn, and Hard Bump.
(5) Click Save.

 
Aggressive Driving shows Hard Brakes when it should show Hard Accel­eration.

•   Ensure that the device is placed in the vehicle in an up or down position and that the LEDs are facing towards the driver, either up or down. The cable should be pointing away from the driver. The device should not be mounted sideways (i.e. where the cable or LEDs point towards the middle of the vehi­cle).

Portal
I can’t log in.

•   Try the “I forgot my username/password” link. If that doesn't work, contact Technical Support at 1-866-294-8637 ext. 3.

 
False events are being reported on Seat Belt, Idling, RPM, and MPG status.

•   (1) Compare VIN number in vehicle to VIN number entered in inthinc® Portal portal (go to Admin > Vehicles).
(2) If VIN numbers don’t match, edit the VIN on the vehicle’s page in the Portal, then re-orient vehicle by pressing the Call button on the tiwi™ device.
If VIN numbers match, then it may be a hardware problem—Contact Techni­cal Support at 1-866-294-8637 ext. 3.

 
I’m receiving errant notifications.

•   Confirm vehicle/driver/unit setup is correct. If no apparent problem is dis­covered, contact Technical Support at 1-866-294-8637 ext. 3.

 
I do not have a RFID card.

•   You will need to manually assign the driver to the vehicle.
(1) Ensure the device is assigned to the vehicle.
(2) Assign the driver to the vehicle.

 
I got an in-cabin/in-car event, but nothing was reported on the portal.

•   To be reported as an event, it needs to last longer than 10 seconds. Was the in-cabin/in-car event long enough to set off a portal event? Look at coaching events.

 
The wrong speed limit is in the system.

•   Speed-by-Street database needs to be updated. Send a change request through the Admin page > Speed By Street tab.

 
Trips are not following maps.

•   The tiwi™ device only sends GPS signals every 15 seconds. On roads with a lot of curves, that could impact how your trip appears on the map.

•   Check for GPS lock. If the device loses GPS lock, it is possible that the trip will show a straight line.

 
The portal inaccurately shows a fleet vehicle off the coast of Africa. Why?

•   This is a satellite glitch. The system temporarily resets to 0’ latitude, 0’ lon­gitude. Log another trip in the vehicle to see if the trip appears on the map correctly.

 
Mileage of trip in vehi­cle does not match por­tal trip.

•   Sometimes, the satellites don’t track properly, or the device loses satellite lock. That will throw off the trip mileage on the portal.

•   Idling Reports are not as accurate as the trip recorded on the portal. The dis­tance shown on reports is an approximation of the trips shown on the portal, and will not always match the map. If you do not have satellite lock, you will lose mileage on a trip, and you will lose reports for that loss of satellite lock.

 
Aggressive driving set­tings are too sensitive or not sensitive enough.

•   Adjust settings on an individual vehicle or device, or edit them collectively. To edit collectively, go to Admin page > Vehicles/Devices tab, then check the applicable boxes and then click Batch Edit button and make your changes. To edit individually, go to Admin page > Vehicles/Devices tab, select a specific vehicle or device, then adjust settings.

 
Miles Per Gallon (MPG) data is wrong. Why?

•   For gasoline, MPG data accuracy is + 30%.

•   For diesel, MPG data is not supported.

 
I’m not getting alerts.

•   You may have configured alerts, but not the contact information, so the alerts aren’t being sent anywhere. Check your My Account page to verify the settings.

•   Also, our speed settings have a 1 MPH buffer. If you set your notification for 5 MPH over the speed limit, the event will trigger at 6 MPH over the speed limit.

 
I’m getting excessive zone alerts.

•   Zone may be too small for GPS accuracy. Widen the zone with an approxi­mate 30-foot buffer on all sides of a zone to allow for GPS inaccuracies.

 
I’m getting excessive speeding violations.

•   You might be experiencing GPS inaccuracies that would account for the excessive speeding alert.

 
I’m getting a high num­ber of speeding notifica­tions.

•   Check your speeding sensitivity settings.

 
Live Fleet is not showing all vehicles.

•   Currently, the portal displays a maximum of 100 vehicles.

 
I have a vehicle with multiple drivers logged in to it.

•   You may have two or more drivers associated with one vehicle.

•   You may have moved a driver from one vehicle to another.

 
Multiple trips under same ID.

•   Check to ensure drivers are logging in before starting a trip, or that your driver is logging out after completing a trip.

 
Some vehicles are get­ting MPG data and some aren’t.

•   Related to wrong VIN number. Ensure VIN number in vehicle matches VIN number in vehicle’s page in Portal.
If any correction is made, then you will have to re-orient the device.

 
Some vehicles are get­ting notifications and some aren’t.

•   The wrong VIN might be associated with the vehicle. Ensure VIN number in vehicle matches VIN number in vehicle’s page in Portal.
If the VIN is incorrect, re-enter the correct VIN, then re-orient the device.

 
I don’t see the changes I’ve made.

•   You need to press Save before changing pages in the portal.

•   Once you save your changes, log out of the portal, then log back in.

 
Tampering Events are not appearing on the portal.

•   Contact Technical Support at 1-866-294-8637 ext. 3.

 
The end trip is not showing up on the por­tal.

•   Check for tampering notifications or that the device is unplugged.

 
Trips are not showing up on the portal

•   Check under the vehicle’s specific page on the portal. If they are not listed under the vehicle’s specific page, contact Technical Support at 1-866-294-8637 ext. 3.

 
Events that I set up are not being sent to the portal. Ex. I have a Red Flag set to send a notifi­cation for anything 5 MPH over, but my sensi­tivity settings are set to 10 MPH over on the device.

•   You will not get speeding alerts for Red Flags set BELOW speeding sensitivity, though you will get speeding alerts for Red Flags set OVER the speeding sensi­tivity.

 
I’m getting the wrong time stamp.
 

•   Check time zone setting:
(1) Got to Admin > User.
(2) Select an individual driver and then click Edit.
(3) Choose new time zone from the drop-down list and then click Save.
