Set Up In-Cab Mentoring

You can set in-cab mentoring by setting the sensitivity levels of vehicle movements (including speed, acceleration, hard turns and hard verticals, such as going over a speed bump). By setting speed and sensitivity preferences, you indicate which events will be detected and how they will be reported. You can keep the default selections initially and adjust them later if you find reporting to be too sensitive or not sensitive enough.

The following are rules about the Speed & Sensitivity options you should know.

Changes to sensitivity levels do not take effect until the vehicle is powered on again. Changes are then reflected on your tiwiPro™ Portal account profile.

Seat belt mentoring cannot be turned off. Seat Belt Safety is designed to ensure seat belt usage at all times. It starts at over 10 mph. If your tiwi™ device detects that a seat belt is not fastened, an alarm sounds to remind the driver to buckle up. The grace period for a violation is 10 seconds. This is only applicable if you have a configuration file on your device. If the warning is ignored for 10 seconds or more, you will be sent an alert.

Speed mentoring cannot be turned off, but you can increase the speeding threshold on this tab. Mentoring starts at 1 mph greater than the speeding threshold. Grace period for a violation is 15 seconds. The mentoring audio will play (in seconds) at 10, 20, 30, 40, 70, 100, 130, and every 30 seconds thereafter until the speed is within the allowable limit.

Aggressive Driving Behavior Mentoring informs drivers when their driving practices can be improved. Hard braking, sharp turns, and unnecessary acceleration are all considered to be “aggressive” driving behavior. When the driver engages in such a behavior, your tiwi™ device will give the driver immediate feedback by audio and visual notifications to correct the aggressive behavior and increase the safety of the driver.

Note: Aggressive Driving Behavior is interpretive, not statistical. Our data is subjective; it is entered and monitored by you, which makes you the ultimate judge on acceptable behavior from your driver.

Speed-by-Street is a unique function that keeps track of your vehicle’s speed in real time. When a driver goes above the posted speed limit, tiwi™ provides immediate driver feedback for the driver to reduce speed. If the speed limit continues to be violated, an alert is then sent to the system owner or manager by whichever methods you choose: text message, phone call or e-mail. You can also set a speed allowance that fits your preferences.

Aggressive Driving Sensor has voice alarm:   

•   Hard brakes usually do not need to be modified between different vehicle types.

•   Hard turns may need to be tuned slightly.

•   Hard bumps are vehicle specific - box vans with windshield in front of front wheels are the most sensitive.

•   Accelerations that are considered dangerous may be impossible to generate on some vehicles even at the most sensitive setting.
