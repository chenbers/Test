Update a Device

Note: Devices are added to your system by an inthinc professional at the time of purchase. But you can update or edit those devices as needed to maintain correct associations with vehicles and users.

Update the device details or Speed & Sensitivity in the system as needed. 

1.   Go to Admin page > Devices tab.


 Figure 28:  Devices page 


2.   Locate the device you want to update in the list, then click the hot link in the Device ID column. 

 
The Edit Device page opens with the Details tab selected.

 

 

 


 Figure 29:  Details tab on Edit Device page


3.   Make the changes necessary on the Details tab. For example, you may need to update the E-Call Phone number. Fields marked with an asterisk [*] are mandatory.

 
CAUTION!  The IMEI, SIM, and Device Phone numbers are specific to the tiwi™ device and should not be changed. Changes to these fields may cause the device to stop working.


a.   Under Device Information, update the fields as needed.

 
•   Device ID.

 
•   IMEI (International Mobile Equipment Identity number found on the tiwi™ label): This number is unique to every cell device and can be used to stop communication from tiwi™ in the event it is stolen.

 
•   SIM Card (A memory card inserted into your device by manufacturing).

 
•   Device Phone: This number is automatically assigned. 

 
•   E-Call phone: This is the number that is called when you push the green button on your device. 


b.   Under Device Profile, choose a status: New, Active, or Inactive.


c.   Under Device Assignment, click Assign. Select a vehicle for this device.


4.   Click the Speed & Sensitivity tab (Figure 30 on page 30), then set in-cab mentoring preferences for vehicle move­ments (speed, acceleration, hard turns and hard verticals, such as going over a speed bump). These settings deter­mine if and when audio mentoring will occur. Adjust them later if you find reporting to be too sensitive or not sensitive enough.


 

  

   

   
   
Note: Changes to Sensitivity Levels do not take effect until the vehicle is powered on again. Changes are then reflected on your inthinc® Portal account profile.




  


 


 

  

   

   
   
Note: Speed mentoring cannot be turned off, but you can increase the speeding threshold on this tab. Mentoring starts at 1 mph greater than the speeding threshold. Grace period for a violation is 15 sec­onds. The mentoring audio will play (in seconds) at 10, 20, 30, 40, 70, 100, 130, and every 30 seconds thereafter until the speed is within the allowable limit.




  


 


 

  

   

   
   
Note: Seat belt mentoring cannot be turned off. It starts at over 10 mph. The grace period for a viola­tion is 10 seconds. This is only applicable if you have a configuration file on your device.




  


 


5.   Click Save.
