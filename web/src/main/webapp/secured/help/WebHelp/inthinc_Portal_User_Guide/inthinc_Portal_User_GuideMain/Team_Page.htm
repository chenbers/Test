Team Page

The Team Page (Figure 3) is typically accessible by Team Managers and those that they report to—such as Fleet Manag­ers, Division Managers, or Section Heads. The Team Page provides team information in a variety of collections or views. The available views are: "Driver Statistics", "Trips", "Stops", "Live Team", "Overall Score", "Driving Style", and "Speed".

 

Figure 4: View options for Team Page

Driver Statistics

The Driver Statistics view of the Team Page also servers as the Team Manager Home Page (see Figure 3). This view dis­plays a list of drivers on a team with individual statistics.

Trips

The Trips view of the Team Page (Figure 5) can be displayed in one of three standard Google Maps ways:

•   Map view shows street names (default view)

•   Satellite view is an aerial photograph

•   Hybrid view is the Map view superimposed on the Satellite view

When viewing trips, you decide which day or group of days to view by choosing a range option (upper-right corner).

Every driver in the list is assigned a letter and each trip is numbered. So a specific trip can then be laid out using one driver letter, one trip number, and however many starts, stops, idle time, and alerts occurred during the trip.

Stops

The Stops view of the Team Page (Figure 7) displays a numbered list of vehicle stops for the team that occurred during the selected time frame. This spreadsheet-like display includes a stop location, arrival and departure times, idle time statistics, and drive time for each trip. It also includes summaries at the bottom of the screen.

Figure 7: Stops view of Team Page

 

Live Team

The Live Team view of the Team Page (Figure 8) helps you determine the location of your fleet at a glance. The Live Team view uses Google Maps and has three viewing options of its own: (1)Map shows street names (default view); Satel­lite is an aerial photograph; and (3) Hybrid is the Map view superimposed on the Satellite view. You can use the high­lighted drop-down list to choose one of these views. Each vehicle is represented in the maps by a colored icon overlay.

Figure 8: Live Team view of Team Page

Overall Score

The Overall Score view of the Team Page (Figure 9) provides a pie-chart version of the running Overall Team Score dis­played at the top of most pages. Score brackets are represented by different colors in the legend and in the pie chart so that at a glance you can see where most of the team’s scores fall.

 

Figure 9: Overall Score view of Team Page

Driving Style

The Driving Style view of the Team Page (Figure 10) provides a bar-chart version of the consolidated team statistics. This shows the distribution of aggressive driving events by type and severity, letting you see at a glance the types of driving behaviors that are most common for the team. When you point the mouse at a specific bar, the exact percentage used to create that bar displays.

Figure 10: Driving Style view of Team Page

Speed

The Speed view of the Team Page (Figure 11) includes a combination of display methods: Speeding statistics for the team are displayed as a numeric score, a bar chart, a pie chart, and a spreadsheet.

Figure 11: Speed view of Team Page

View All Trips page

When you click the View All Trips link in either the Driver Trips page and Vehicle Trips page, the initial display in the map is the last trip taken.  

To view a different trip, you can select it in the table. You may need to use the navigation bar below the Trips table OR change the dates in the Data Range fields.

Trips table displays information regarding the last four trips, including the GPS status during the trip:

Good GPS

Bad GPS

Unknown GPS - Trip in progress or latent end of trip attribute

The inthinc® device tracks the GPS quality every second. At the end of the trip a GPS percentage notification is sent to the portal that indicates the percentage of time the GPS data is locked. The quality is calculated using the following formula:

goodPoints = gpsPercentage * tripDuration

goodTrip = (goodPoints / (tripDuration - 90 seconds)

If a goodTrip is greater than 90%, then the trip is considered good. Ninety seconds are subtracted to allow the GPS some time to acquire a lock. On trips less than 90 seconds the inthinc® device uses the gpsPercentage without any modifica­tions.

The navigation bar below the table allows you to scroll through previous trips within the specified range.

Data Range defaults to the last seven days. Use the calendar to select dates, then click Update Data Range to refresh the screen.

Stats section displays totals for trips taken within the data range.

Events section displays any driving notifications, such as speeding, hard brakes, hard accelerations, etc.

Settings section is used to set preferences.
